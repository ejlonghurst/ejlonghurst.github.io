INTRODUCTION

If you like birds, and dogs, donkeys and cats, and all kinds of animals, love them even; if you've walked and run among trees, and climbed them, or wanted to, seen rivers and the sea and swum in them, or wanted to; if you like being out of doors, or maybe love it, then the Earthkin books are definitely for you. I began writing these books because I wanted to create a thrilling adventure story for all young people who like nature, or love it. It was supposed to be one book, but the story grew itself into two books, and then three! I love writing stories. Earthkin Book 1 was only half written when I began reading it to young people in my family. To my delight they got so excited and involved in the story and what might happen next, and the characters became so real and almost like part of the family, that I just had to go on writing it. My inspirations were the great spirit of adventure of young people, and their love and care for nature. We all share this amazing world and I wanted to write an adventure story to inspire and entertain all the young people who want to be the guardians and helpers of nature. We all know about people doing damage to the Earth, to forests and rivers and the sea, but millions of us want to help nature and do good. The Earthkin books are about a perilous adventure to do just that, about courage and daring, friendships and upsets, crazy risks, fighting, chasing, trusting, racing, in the fight against evil powers that want to destroy everything. The central characters are four young people. Georgia and Tia are sisters, twins, 14 years old. They live in a small town somewhere in England. Matild and Jaren, sister and brother, 15 and 13, live thousands of miles away in a hot country with rainforests. Though on opposite sides of the world these four young people share a great love for nature, and are destined to meet and embark on a huge adventure full of dangers, thrills, triumphs and disasters, a unique quest to defend and protect the Earth.
